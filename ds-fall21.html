Where I'm From

I am from a toy train set,
Five hundred plastic and metal parts
All dismantled
I’m from the I-can’t-do-its and the hey-guess-whats
In roughly equal measure.

I’m from the traffic light at the cornfield
pumpkin muffins and Victor records
From covered bridges
With old broken bottles underneath
And tiny frogs swimming among them
I raised them well.

I am from getting tailgated by a Mercedes
Riding in the back of a ’93 Saturn
Because we are stuck behind
A quarry truck.

I’m from the first good wineberries of the season
From the thorns
And the cries of birds overhead
because they wanted them too.

I was once from Phoenix Books
And the path along the canal
Where the ducks come up to beg.
I’m from drastic measures
And will-you-be-mines
And Route 22.

I am from “V. H. Burdorf: Meats”
And jars of buttons
From Shahnama and General Electric
From Varney, the Reading, and Delta
Out in the Grand Beater
To the city in the mountains
To the bricks in the valleys.

I am from rust and dust
I am from glass and paper
I am the wandering Pennsylvanian
I am from everyone who used to be there*
